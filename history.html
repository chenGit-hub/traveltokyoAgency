History of Tokyo

Discover the rich history of Japan's vibrant capital.

Timeline of Tokyo's History

1603: Edo Period

Tokyo, then known as Edo, became the seat of the Tokugawa shogunate, marking the start of its historical prominence.

1868: Renamed Tokyo

Following the Meiji Restoration, Edo was renamed Tokyo, meaning "Eastern Capital."

1923: Great Kanto Earthquake

A devastating earthquake destroyed much of the city but led to a modern rebuilding effort.

1945: WWII Aftermath

Tokyo was heavily bombed during World War II, but its post-war reconstruction transformed it into a global metropolis.

Interesting Facts About Tokyo

  • Tokyo is home to over 14 million people in the city proper and 37 million in the metropolitan area.
  • It hosted the Olympics twice: in 1964 and 2021.
  • The city features more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city in the world.
  • Tokyo was originally a small fishing village named Edo.
